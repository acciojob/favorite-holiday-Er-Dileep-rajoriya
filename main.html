Paris, France


Paris, the capital city of France, is known for its art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. The city's 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Paris is known for its cafe culture and landmarks like the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and the Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral.

  1. Eiffel Tower: The iconic symbol of Paris, offering spectacular views from the top.
  2. Louvre Museum: Home to famous artworks such as the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo.
  3. Notre-Dame Cathedral: A masterpiece of Gothic architecture, known for its stunning stained-glass windows.
  4. Sacré-Cœur Basilica: A Roman Catholic church perched on top of Montmartre, offering panoramic views of the city.
  5. Champs-Élysées: One of the world's most famous avenues, lined with shops, cafes, and theatres.
  • Croissants: A buttery, flaky pastry that is a breakfast staple in France.
  • Baguette: A long, thin loaf of French bread that is a must-try for any visitor.
  • Macarons: Colorful meringue-based confections with a creamy filling.
  • Crème Brûlée: A rich custard dessert with a caramelized sugar top.
  • Escargot: Snails cooked in garlic butter, a traditional French delicacy.
